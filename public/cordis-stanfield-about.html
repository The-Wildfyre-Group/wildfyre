HOW I GOT HERE / WHERE I HOPE TO GO / MOTIVATING FORCES

Advise early-stage companies in areas of fundraising, corporate structuring, revenue viability, research-driven concept validation, niche product development, and market positioning. Act as lead designer and front-end developer specializing in information architecture.



HOW I GOT HERE...


I’ve always felt that my personality and my interests have been consistent over time. I generally, not by design (that's Khadijah's job), like and pursue feats that are difficult and not always popularly pursued by the general population with vigor. Finance, web development, and entrepreneurship are all viewed somewhat similarly: arduous, technical, too demanding, and too risky. Combine those with my hobby of choice, long distance running, and you have a recipe for a guy that prides himself on doing things other people can’t, won’t, and don’t do.

Although I pride myself on being technically capable, inwardly, I feel my talent as an innovator, thinker, and motivator rivals or evens exceeds my ability to get things done. This realization, or some unfounded irrational hubris, lead to the belief that Khadijah and I could combine technical and innovative qualities, with business backgrounds, to develop a collective of companies that spread like “Wildfyre.”




WHERE I HOPE TO GO...


In the short run, I hope to improve as a co-founder, in all areas, such as: creativity, administration, management, marketing, networking and technical ability. My ability to improve as an individual will drive my ability to help Wildfyre improve as a team and an organization.



MOTIVATING FORCES...


In the most generic of terms, I’ve always liked ownership and I’ve been an athlete. The life lessons that I learned as a competitive athlete have helped me be a competitive entrepreneur. Simply put, my motivation is win. I want to encourage others to win by becoming entrepreneurs.

In my quest to be an evangelist for entrepreneurship, I often find myself repeating something like this: every business that you’ve ever patronized was started by someone who not only had an idea, but also had the courage pursue that idea as a business.

My goal is to be successful enough to be a role model to everyone.
