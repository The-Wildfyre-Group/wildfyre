HOW I GOT HERE / WHERE I HOPE TO GO / MOTIVATING FORCES

Advise early-stage companies in areas of fundraising, corporate structuring, revenue viability, research-driven concept validation, niche product development, and market positioning. Act as lead designer and front-end developer specializing in information architecture.



HOW I GOT HERE...


I was an ambitious little thing out of Southern California. I came to Berkeley wanting to study Computer Science but NCAA Div I hoops wasn't having it (not on their dime at least). I opted for Economics -- to think, reason, argue, articulate, and write. #firstlove. I sprinkled this with a double-major's-worth of courses in english, rhetoric, film, history, finance, accounting, and entrepreneurship. Tech was purused through summer internships until an enginnering friend told me that invesment bankers made 6-figures out of school. #mindblown. Then I sold my soul to the devil and set targets on 'Banking' for 'all the same reasons'.

I went to NY, played hard, worked hard, and left 2 years later for 'all the same reasons'. I was desperate for meaning, color, balance, and something wordly...something bigger than myself. I landed at the investment house of the World Bank and was fortunate to work in an environment of unrivalled cultural diversity with some of the smartest and most thoughtful individuals I didn't know could co-exist in an uber-successful private-equity-like setting. It was perfect. #bestever.

Then I left. Somewhere in the middle I realized there was only three things I loved -- Design, Technology, and Health & Fitness. It wasn't enough to have them as hobbies. I had this concept of "I'd Rather Be...". It was the mental inefficency and pre-occupation with what you'd rather be doing at any point in time. It was a mental check, and I wasn't passing. I had a vision of asking myself this question, filling in the blank and being exactly where I wanted to be, doing exactly what I wanted to be doing...and I ended up here.



WHERE I HOPE TO GO...


I'm a woman of contractions. My frugality rivals my ambition for material wealth. My humbility rivals my arrogance. I intend to go everwhere but plan to be nowhere. "Expect everything and need nothing," is my personal mantra. Those who know me best would describe me as someone who lives in the wind and is impulsively of-the-moment. I go where I feel called to be, I do what I find purpose in doing. That is where I hope to always live. I've grown fond of being certain of my faith but uncertain of the journey. For me, success is presence of mind and freedom of choice. I am a firm beliver that you must already be where you hope to go should you ever want to end up there. I am pursuing inspired visions, fleeting thoughts, passion projects, validated enterprises. I 'arrived' when I decided I did. I'm here and it is my daily spiritual practice to continue to be here. I'm audacious enough to believe that I can have it all. That everything in life is a matter of want, desire, and faith in the great promise.



MOTIVATING FORCES...


I work 12-18 hours a day but it's not work. I am connected to my craft as a creator and producer of things. My action and my work is inspired, it was a calling.

HOW I GOT HERE...


I’ve always felt that my personality and my interests have been consistent over time. I generally, not by design (that's Khadijah's job), like and pursue feats that are difficult and not always popularly pursued by the general population with vigor. Finance, web development, and entrepreneurship are all viewed somewhat similarly: arduous, technical, too demanding, and too risky. Combine those with my hobby of choice, long distance running, and you have a recipe for a guy that prides himself on doing things other people can’t, won’t, and don’t do.

Although I pride myself on being technically capable, inwardly, I feel my talent as an innovator, thinker, and motivator rivals or evens exceeds my ability to get things done. This realization, or some unfounded irrational hubris, lead to the belief that Khadijah and I could combine technical and innovative qualities, with business backgrounds, to develop a collective of companies that spread like “Wildfyre.”




WHERE I HOPE TO GO...


In the short run, I hope to improve as a co-founder, in all areas, such as: creativity, administration, management, marketing, networking and technical ability. My ability to improve as an individual will drive my ability to help Wildfyre improve as a team and an organization.



MOTIVATING FORCES...


In the most generic of terms, I’ve always liked ownership and I’ve been an athlete. The life lessons that I learned as a competitive athlete have helped me be a competitive entrepreneur. Simply put, my motivation is win. I want to encourage others to win by becoming entrepreneurs.

In my quest to be an evangelist for entrepreneurship, I often find myself repeating something like this: every business that you’ve ever patronized was started by someone who not only had an idea, but also had the courage pursue that idea as a business.

My goal is to be successful enough to be a role model to everyone.
